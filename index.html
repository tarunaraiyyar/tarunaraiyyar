What Makes a Viewer Watch? Unpacking Pre-Roll Ad Engagement

Regression Modeling · 10,000 viewer records · South Korean Video Platform

Business Problem

A video platform needs to know which content genres and brand categories keep viewers from skipping ads. The goal is to inform smarter ad placement and give advertisers the data to justify premium pricing.

The data revealed that not all ad slots are equal. Where an ad plays and what brand it represents matters far more than most platforms account for. Running four models on 10,000 viewer records confirmed these effects hold up under rigorous testing.

Sports content drove the highest completion lift of 23.8pp. The recommendation: price sports slots at a premium.
Finance ads outperformed every other category with a 41.3% completion rate. High-stakes brands hold viewer attention.
Platform is the biggest confounder. PC Web users completed 31.6% of ads vs 8.9% on Mobile App. Device targeting is non-negotiable.

02 Factor Analysis

What Actually Drives Music Listening? A Factor Analysis of Consumer Motivation

Exploratory Factor Analysis · 27 Survey Items · Multiple Regression

Business Problem

A music platform wants to go beyond demographic data to understand what psychologically drives listening behavior. Knowing the real motivations behind consumption is what makes content strategy and marketing campaigns actually land.

The data revealed three distinct motivational dimensions behind music listening. Not all of them drive consumption equally, and that distinction has real implications for how platforms and artists should market themselves.

Social identity is the strongest driver of listening time (β=1.77). People who share, discuss, and wear their music taste listen significantly more.
Emotional resonance, despite feeling central to music, does not predict listening volume. It shapes virality, not habit.
The recommendation: invest in community and self-expression features. Curated playlists for moods and activities serve the background listener, another proven consumption driver.

03 K-Means Clustering

Who Are Your Cable Customers Really? A Segmentation Study for Smarter Marketing

K-Means Clustering · Video, Internet and Phone Spending Data

Business Problem

A cable operator was treating all subscribers the same. To grow engagement and long-term value, they needed to understand who their customers actually are and what distinct spending patterns and needs each group has.

The data revealed six meaningfully different customer segments shaped primarily by age and income. A key decision in this project was choosing not to standardize the data, which would have distorted the phone variable and masked real behavioral differences between subscribers.

Six segments emerged, each with a distinct profile. The highest-value group, Family-Focused Heavy Watchers, are the heaviest cable TV consumers with the largest households and highest income.
Phone service is weak across all six segments. That is not a data anomaly; it is a clear upsell opportunity.
The recommendation: personalize pricing and content offers by segment rather than by service tier alone.

04 Predictive Modeling

Less is More or Less is a Bore? Device Behavior and Subscriber Churn

Logistic Regression + K-Means Clustering · 105,000+ Subscriber Records

Business Problem

A digital media publisher was losing subscribers and needed to understand which user behaviors predicted churn. The business question was simple: does reading across multiple devices actually build loyalty, or is it just a usage pattern?

The data revealed that device behavior is a stronger churn signal than most publishers track. Multichannel readers are not just more engaged; they are meaningfully less likely to leave. That finding held up across both clustering and logistic regression.

Multichannel users churn at 0.65% vs 1.1% for single-channel. Broader device engagement builds real loyalty, not just session count.
Mobile-only users are the highest churn risk at 1.54%. Tablet-primary multichannel users are the most loyal at 0.57%.
The recommendation: prioritize cross-device features and use app adoption nudges for mobile-only users before they churn.
